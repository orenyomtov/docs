---
title: "Set up a custom domain for your Mintlify docs"
sidebarTitle: "Custom Domain"
description: "Point a custom domain or subdomain at your Mintlify docs site by adding a CNAME record and configuring the domain in your project settings."
---

By default, your Mintlify docs are served at `{subdomain}.mintlify.app`. A custom domain gives your docs a branded URL — something like `docs.yourcompany.com` — so readers stay in your domain ecosystem and your documentation feels like a natural extension of your product.

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:

- **A domain you own.** You can use a root domain (`yourcompany.com`) or, more commonly, a subdomain (`docs.yourcompany.com`).
- **Access to your DNS provider.** You'll need to add a CNAME record through whoever manages your domain's DNS — this might be your domain registrar (e.g. Namecheap, GoDaddy) or a DNS service (e.g. Cloudflare, AWS Route 53).

## Configure in Mintlify

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your project settings">
    Go to [https://dashboard.mintlify.com](https://dashboard.mintlify.com), select your project, then navigate to **Settings** → **Domain**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your custom domain">
    Type your desired domain into the domain field — for example, `docs.yourcompany.com` — and click **Save**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the CNAME target">
    After saving, Mintlify will display the CNAME target you need to point your DNS record at. Copy this value — you'll use it in the next step.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Update DNS

With the CNAME target from Mintlify in hand, log in to your DNS provider and add a new DNS record:

| Type  | Name   | Value                     | TTL  |
|-------|--------|---------------------------|------|
| CNAME | `docs` | `{mintlify-cname-target}` | 3600 |

- Set **Name** (sometimes called "Host" or "Subdomain") to the subdomain prefix you want — `docs` if you're using `docs.yourcompany.com`.
- Set **Value** (sometimes called "Points to" or "Target") to the CNAME target Mintlify provided.
- TTL can be left at your provider's default or set to `3600` (one hour).

<Note>
  DNS changes can take anywhere from a few minutes to **48 hours** to propagate worldwide, depending on your DNS provider and existing TTL values. It's normal for your custom domain to not resolve immediately after adding the record.
</Note>

<Note>
  Mintlify automatically provisions and renews a TLS/SSL certificate for your custom domain — no certificate setup or renewal is needed on your end. Your docs will be served over HTTPS as soon as DNS propagates.
</Note>

## Verify

Once your DNS changes have propagated, visit your custom domain in a browser (e.g. `https://docs.yourcompany.com`). You should see your Mintlify docs site load over HTTPS with your custom URL in the address bar.

If the domain isn't resolving after 48 hours, double-check:

- The CNAME record is saved correctly in your DNS provider (no typos in the target).
- You're pointing the right subdomain prefix (e.g. `docs`, not `www`).
- The domain entered in the Mintlify dashboard exactly matches what you configured in DNS.
